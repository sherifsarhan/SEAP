Welcome to SEAP

American Professionals

SEAP is where talented individuals meet opportunity. We help bring together a group of professionals from different industries and disciplines that are well rounded and provide a supportive environment for everyone. At SEAP, we collaborate -- and that drives us to excellence. We are constantly improving by growing our network and giving back more than ever to the community.

About SEAP

SEAP seeks to provide a platform for Egyptian Americans from all around the country to benefit themselves and benefit the community at large. The SEAP community aims to inspire, empower, and enable Egyptian American youth by focusing on professional development, community development, and international outreach.

Excellent Networking

Establishing a high quality network of Egyptian American professionals. SEAP is the first ever and most established professional society for Egyptian Americans as of today. Our network consists of very intellegent, hard-working individuals.

Core Building

Utilizing network resources to contribute to the local communities of our members. We can empower individuals with potential can be given an opportunity through a strong and widespread SEAP network.

Inspiring Future Generations

Providing a platform for our network to enhance the professional advancement of youth in Egypt. SEAP is meant to be a platform for now and the future. Investing in our youth is crucial to the continued existence of SEAP.

Social and Professional Gathering

Networking events, conferences, workshops, and mentorship programs that reflect the interests of our members. Work with peers and mentors to develop your skills, and help others develop their skills in a culturally familiar and trustworthy environment.

Career Advancement

Meet people who can help you with your next career move and help you surface a new business or professional opportunity, tap into a network of subject matter experts in different industries and professional disciplines that you can engage with.

Personal Bonding

Make lifelong connections at SEAP whether it may be personal or professional. Not only is it important to be skilled to be successful in the workforce, it is also important to be in a good mental, physical, and emotional state.

About Our Team

Yomna Sarhan Founder

Yomna graduated from Georgetown University with a Bachelor’s in International Politics and a certificate in African Studies. She was a coordinator for the Leadership in Education and Development program at the American University in Cairo. Yomna also worked at the Middle East Program of the Woodrow Wilson Center, the Moroccan-American Center for Policy, Georgetown’s Berkley Center for Religion, Peace, and World Affairs, interned with the United Nations Non-Governmental Liaison Service, and was a Progam Associate at the Aspen Institute Communications & Society Program. Yomna also has a Master’s in Public Policy from George Washington University.

Yehia Mokhtar Co-Founder

Born and raised in Alexandria, Egypt, Yehia graduated with a degree in political science and international political economy from the University of Texas at Dallas. He now works as a Risk Analyst for the Middle East department at Kroll Compliance, the world’s leading risk consulting firm. Previously, he interned at the Project on Middle East Democracy in Washington, D.C. and the Egyptian Center for Public Policy Studies and the Egyptian Council for Foreign Affairs in Cairo. During his free time, Yehia enjoys playing soccer, following world news, and shamelessly scoping out the best Shawerma places in town.

Mohamed Elshazzly

Mohamed is currently a medical student at Campbell University. He previously served as a post-baccalaureate fellow at the National Institutes of Health, and held research positions at the American University in Cairo & 57357 Cancer Hospital. He is the co-founder of the non-governmental organization, SuperMuslims, and served as previous president of Resala (AUC), a community service organization.

Lydia Bassaly

Lydia was born and raised in Cairo, Egypt and moved to Atlanta, Georgia in 2003. She graduated from Georgia State University in 2014 with a Bachelor’s degree in Public Policy, concentrating in Nonprofit Leadership and minoring in Psychology. Upon graduating, she returned to Cairo to volunteer with various nonprofits and an orphanage. She also used the opportunity to film and co-produce a documentary on the general sense of hopelessness many feel about the country’s state, yet the select few individuals that arise above such hopelessness and work for a better society. After five months in Cairo, Lydia conducted an internship at a nonprofit in Washington, D.C, and is currently preparing for her graduate studies at Columbia University, NYC for a Master’s in International Affairs, concentrating in Humanitarian Policy and Human Rights. When not traveling, eating, or hiking mountains, she enjoys reading particularly on human rights issues in the Middle East.

Maged Abdelsalam

With a focus on a human centered approach to design, and a passion for social impact in education, Maged has a strong interest in technology as a tool for social innovation. He earned Masters of Arts degree in Social Design at Maryland Collage Institute of Arts, Bachelor of Art in Visual Arts from the American University of Cairo and a Certificate in Web design from the University of Washington in Seattle. He worked as a visual designer, designing brands, coding sites, and directing Ad campaigns with young startups such as Almaqarr and Zacomments, non-profit organizations such as Baltimore Campaign for Grade Level Reading and government such as Baltimore City Schools, in both Egypt and US.
